Section 3: How to get your first credit card?

Getting Started

In order to get your first credit card you need to apply in the respective credit card's application link. You either get approved or rejected for the credit card and many different factors affect this decision. Some credit cards require a good credit score, a good income, and a great credit history in order to get accepted for the credit card. For beginners into their credit journey, there are applications for student credit cards or secured credit cards that are much easier to get accepted for.

Credit Card Application

The credit card application consists of questions ranging from your personal information such as birthday, social security, name, etc. and information about your income as well. When you are ready to apply, you have to go to the application link for a certain credit card and fill out this information truthfully. You will usually receive an approval for your credit card instantly or sometimes it takes 24 hours.

Beginner Credit Cards

To get your first ever credit card, it is recommended that you don't apply to cards that have many benefits or travel points yet. To get accepted for those, you need a well established credit score and you need a beginner credit card such as a Student credit Card or a secured credit card that almost guarantees ecceptance in order to build up your credit score. The next tab on this website tutorial has many beginner friendly credit cards as well as tips when applying to them. After completing this tutorial and answering all of the quizzes, feel free to head over to that tab and get started with your credit journey with your first credit card. Don't worry, credit cards are not that intimidating if you know how to manage your budget and follow the GOLDEN RULE OF CREDIT CARDS.

Advice:

  • ALWAYS FOLLOW THE GOLDEN RULE OF CREDIT CARDS
  • If you have never had a credit card before, it is suggested that you apply to a student or secured credit card
  • The income you put in your application matters for more advanced credit cards, the more income you earn, generally the higher the credit line you will receive
  • Always pay off your credit card after each statement month in order to avoid extra fees and interest
  • Refer to this tutorial when something is unclear about credit cards
  • Build a relationship with your bank that you have a debit card in. They will help you get your first credit card, but always check the benefits and fees of the credit card in order to make sure you're getting the best deal.
